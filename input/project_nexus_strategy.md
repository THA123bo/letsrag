## 1. Executive Summary & Vision
Project Nexus represents the most significant overhaul to our internal knowledge infrastructure since 2018. Over the past five years, our workforce has relied on a legacy, strictly lexical search system based on BM25. While reliable, this legacy architecture failed to capture semantic intent, leading to severe productivity bottlenecks where engineers spent an average of 1.4 hours per day searching for technical documentation.

Project Nexus is our strategic answer: a next-generation Semantic Search and Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) ecosystem. The vision is to integrate a 7-Billion parameter reasoning model directly into the search pipeline, capable of synthesizing direct, context-aware answers across the entire corporate knowledge base, drastically reducing search latency and improving accuracy.

## 2. Technical Architecture Choices
The foundation of Project Nexus relies on a multi-stage retrieval process coupled with a generative backend.

### 2.1 Embedding Strategy
After extensive evaluation of proprietary and open-source embedding vectors, the team has selected the `nomic-embed-text` architecture via LiteLLM/Ollama. This choice provides a 8192-token context window for document chunks and supports highly optimized mathematical operations at the edge, offering a 45% speedup over standard BERT-based sentence transformers.

### 2.2 Generative Backend
Given the constraints imposed by the AI Ethics Policy 2026, which forbids cloud APIs for production tools, Nexus will rely entirely on a self-hosted Qwen-7B parameter model. The model will be constrained by the "Quantum-Ethic-Filter v1" during the initial staging phase, with a mandatory upgrade planned before the final production rollout.
